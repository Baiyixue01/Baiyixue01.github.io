Abstract

Parametric Computer-Aided Design (CAD) is foundational to modern engineering and manufacturing. However, most existing 3D deep learning frameworks focus on boundary representations (B-rep) or point clouds, ignoring the sequential, user-driven **operation-level construction history** that defines authentic CAD modeling.

In this work, we introduce Op-CAD, a large-scale dataset featuring over **128,000 comprehensive operation-level CAD modeling sequences**. Furthermore, we propose a novel reasoning framework that leverages **Chain-of-Operation** prompting and rigorous geometric evaluation metrics (such as edge-aware intersection-over-union) to benchmark boundary accuracy and operational consistency.

Key Features of Op-CAD

128K+ Instances

One of the largest repositories of richly annotated parametric models, capturing intricate topological structures and engineering intent.

Operation-Level

Full construction histories including 2D sketches, extrusions, chamfers, fillets, and complex Boolean manipulations.

Precise Evaluation

Provides rigorous CAD-specific metrics tailored for edge-sensitive geometry and geometric operation fidelity.
